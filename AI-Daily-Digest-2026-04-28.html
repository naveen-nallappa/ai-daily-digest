Microsoft / OpenAI Joint Statement

Microsoft & OpenAI Restructure Partnership — Cloud Exclusivity Ends, Multi-Cloud Unlocked, Revenue Share Capped Through 2030, AGI Clause Removed; License Through 2032 Now Non-Exclusive

Microsoft and OpenAI on Monday April 27 announced a sweeping restructuring of their multi-billion-dollar partnership. Microsoft remains OpenAI's "primary" cloud partner and retains a license to OpenAI's IP and models through 2032 — but the license is now non-exclusive, freeing OpenAI to ship products on AWS, Google Cloud, and other clouds. OpenAI's revenue-share payments to Microsoft will continue through 2030 but are now subject to a total cap and decoupled from technology milestones; Microsoft will no longer pay OpenAI any revenue share on OpenAI products it sells. The contractual "AGI clause" — which previously could have triggered Microsoft losing access if OpenAI declared AGI — was removed entirely. The reset clears the legal overhang from OpenAI's $50B Amazon deal and the recently announced Google partnership.

Why it matters: Every wealth and asset management firm with an OpenAI-backed product (Morgan Stanley AI @ MS, JPMorgan LLM Suite/Coach, Citi Sky on Gemini, Schwab Investor AI on its undisclosed stack) just got a structurally cheaper, more flexible procurement environment. Vendor lock-in risk drops; multi-cloud bake-offs become real; and the implied OpenAI ARR ceiling shifts. Procurement and risk teams should re-open last quarter's signed contracts to verify their cloud-portability rights match the new market.
