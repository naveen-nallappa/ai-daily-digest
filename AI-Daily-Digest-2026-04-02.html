OpenAI Pulls the Plug on Sora, Redirects $1M-a-Day Compute Budget to Enterprise AI

OpenAI has officially begun shutting down Sora, its AI video-generation tool, after the app burned through roughly $1 million per day while active users cratered from 1 million to under 500,000. The consumer app shuts down April 26, with the API following in September. CEO Sam Altman is reallocating the freed compute toward coding tools, enterprise products, and a unified "super app" combining ChatGPT with other capabilities.

Why it matters: The Sora shutdown is a clear signal that even the most well-funded AI labs are prioritizing revenue-generating enterprise use cases over consumer moonshots. For wealth managers, this reinforces that the AI tools most likely to endure — and merit client discussion — are those with clear business models and enterprise demand.

State AI Laws Take Effect as White House Pushes for Federal Preemption Framework

As multiple state-level AI laws begin taking effect in 2026, creating a patchwork of compliance requirements, the White House is actively pushing its National AI Policy Framework through Congress. The framework calls for federal preemption of "overly burdensome" state regulations while establishing regulatory sandboxes for AI testing. The Commerce Department has been directed to identify conflicting state laws for referral to the federal task force.

Why it matters: Financial services firms deploying AI across state lines face mounting compliance complexity. A federal preemption framework would simplify deployment of AI-driven advisory, compliance, and risk tools — but until it passes, firms must navigate a growing web of state-by-state requirements.
