Google's TurboQuant Breakthrough Slashes AI Inference Memory Costs

Google's research team unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that dramatically reduces the memory overhead of the KV cache — a key bottleneck in running large language models. The method uses a two-step process combining PolarQuant vector rotation and the Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression method, enabling frontier-class models to run on significantly cheaper hardware.

Why it matters: Lower inference costs directly affect the economics of deploying AI in wealth management.
