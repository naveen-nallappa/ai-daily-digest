Anthropic / Project Deal

Anthropic Publishes "Project Deal" — 69 Employees Hand Their Wallets to Claude Agents; Agents Close 186 Deals Worth $4,000+, Reveal a Material "Agent Quality" Gap Where Losers Don't Realize They Lost

Anthropic on April 24–25 published the results of "Project Deal," a one-week internal experiment that converted its San Francisco office into a Claude-run classified marketplace. Sixty-nine self-selected employees gave their agents $100 each (gift cards) to buy and sell coworkers' personal items. Across four runs in December 2025, agents executed 186 deals worth more than $4,000. Anthropic's punchline: when one party was represented by Claude Opus 4.5 and the other by Haiku 4.5, Opus-side sellers earned ~$2.68 more per item, Opus buyers saved ~$2.45, and Opus users completed roughly 2.07 more deals — yet Haiku-represented users did not notice the disparity.

Why it matters: This is the first credible empirical demonstration that "agent quality" is a real, measurable economic edge — and that the losing side often won't perceive it. For wealth platforms about to deploy client-facing agents (Schwab in June, Citi Sky this summer, Merrill's Meeting Journey already at 18,000 advisors), the diligence question shifts from "do you have an agent?" to "what model backs it, and how do you protect clients negotiating against better-equipped counterparty agents?"
