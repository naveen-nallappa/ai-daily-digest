More Than a Third of Consumers Now Consult ChatGPT and Claude Before Their Advisor

A new McKinsey report finds that more than a third of consumers across every age group are turning to tools like Claude and ChatGPT for investment guidance — and in many cases are consulting the AI tools before meeting with their human advisor. At the same time, just 38% of affluent investors say they are at least somewhat comfortable with AI managing their finances, a slight decline from 39% in 2024, highlighting a trust gap that persists even as usage climbs.

Why it matters: Advisors are increasingly meeting clients who arrive with AI-generated questions, opinions, and even portfolio ideas. This shifts the value of the advisor conversation from "who has the information" to "who can contextualize and vet it." Firms should be training advisors to engage productively with clients' AI-sourced inputs — and using their own AI tools to ensure advisors never walk into a meeting less informed than the chatbot their client just consulted.
